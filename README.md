Part 2: Research Paper DraftTitle: Precision Phonology in the Latent Space: Re-evaluating L2 Tone Acquisition through Audio-JEPA and the Free Energy Principle1. IntroductionThe acquisition of lexical tone by non-tonal L1 speakers presents a fundamental challenge to both cognitive psychology and applied linguistics. Historically, the evaluation of L2 phonological acquisition has relied heavily on acoustic laboratory phonetics, utilizing tools like Dynamic Time Warping (DTW) or Legendre polynomial decompositions of fundamental frequency (F0) contours. While mathematically rigorous, these acoustic metrics suffer from a reductionist bias: they isolate pitch from the entangled semantic, vocalic, and temporal realities of human speech perception.This paper argues that human lexical tone categories are not stored as disembodied acoustic curves, but as high-dimensional, multimodal clusters within a cognitive latent space. To empirically test this, we introduce the UMONS-JEPA Protocol, bridging the gap between classical acoustic control and modern foundation AI. By passing a highly controlled L2 Mandarin acquisition corpus through a pre-trained Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (Audio-JEPA) and analyzing the output via the Free Energy Principle (FEP), we propose a mathematically rigorous method for measuring the true cognitive—rather than purely physical—acquisition of phonological rules.2. Contribution to the Research LandscapeCurrent methodologies in phonetics struggle to separate acoustic motor mimicry (reproducing a sound) from phonological abstraction (understanding a category). Generative AI models (like Autoencoders) mimic traditional acoustic metrics by prioritizing the reconstruction of the physical waveform.This research disrupts the current landscape by replacing waveform reconstruction with Joint-Embedding prediction. Because Audio-JEPA learns by predicting abstract, masked representations of speech, its latent space naturally mirrors the entangled semantic reality of human cognition. Furthermore, by applying the Free Energy Principle—specifically utilizing the Hessian matrix to measure the curvature of categorical "attractor states"—this paper provides the first framework to quantitatively measure cognitive uncertainty and categorical boundaries in L2 learners without relying on subjective human raters or reductionist F0 isolation.3. Research Questions & HypothesesThis study leverages a 30-hour L2 Mandarin corpus, featuring a $2 \times 2$ factorial design of High-Variability Phonetic Training (HVPT) manipulating Talker Variability and Stimuli Richness.RQ1 (Native Topology): Does the latent space of Audio-JEPA naturally formulate discrete, strict categories for L1 Mandarin tones without explicit labels?H1: L1 latent clusters will exhibit deep Free Energy minimums, quantified by a high Hessian determinant $|H|$.RQ2 (L2 Ambiguity): How does L2 tonal ambiguity manifest mathematically in the cognitive architecture?H2: Pre-HVPT L2 productions will form shallow, overlapping valleys with low precision (small Hessian determinant), specifically between Tones 2 and 3.RQ3 (HVPT Efficacy): What are the pedagogical drivers of phonological abstraction?H3: Learners exposed to Multi-Voice HVPT will exhibit a statistically significant increase in categorical precision (Hessian) and a reduction in Euclidean distance to the L1 centroid compared to Single-Voice learners.RQ4 (The Sandhi Dissociation): Can the latent space dissociate sub-phonemic acoustic precision from prosodic rule acquisition?H4: At the T3T3 D1 sandhi position, acoustic deviation (DTW) against the target T3 will remain flat ($d \approx 0$), while Euclidean distance in the JEPA latent space toward the Native T2 centroid will significantly decrease, proving rule acquisition independent of articulatory mimicry.4. Methodology: The UMONS-JEPA ProtocolThe experimental design operates across three interconnected layers to guarantee scientific validity:Acoustic Ground Truth (Praat/MFA): The independent variables are strictly controlled. The UMONS corpus is automatically segmented via the Montreal Forced Aligner (MFA). For diagnostic testing, Praat is utilized to synthesize a mathematically perfect Tone 2 to Tone 3 F0 continuum, controlling for amplitude and duration to prevent AI confounding variables.Cognitive Mapping (Audio-JEPA Inference): The perfectly annotated stimuli are fed into a frozen, pre-trained Audio-JEPA foundation model. A layer-wise probe will determine the optimal hidden layer that maximizes the L1 Silhouette Score. Continuous latent vectors ($x_i \in \mathbb{R}^D$) representing the vowel nuclei are extracted.Free Energy Mathematics (Hessian Extraction): For each learner and session, dimensionality reduction (PCA) and Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage are applied to stabilize the vector arrays. The covariance matrix ($\Sigma$) is calculated, and its inverse is taken to derive the Hessian precision matrix: $H = \Sigma^{-1}$.5. Quantitative and Qualitative AnalysisQuantitative Analysis: The primary statistical evaluation will employ a Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMEM). The dependent variable is the scalar precision (Hessian determinant) of the learner's tonal cluster. Fixed effects include Session (Pre vs. Post), Talker Variability, and Stimuli Richness, with Speaker set as a random effect. False Discovery Rate (FDR) corrections will be applied to all interaction $p$-values.Qualitative Analysis: To contextualize the mathematics, Topological Mapping (t-SNE or UMAP dimensionality reduction) will be used to visualize the FEP landscape. This will allow for qualitative observation of the L2 learner's trajectory—visually tracking how a diffuse, amorphous cloud of vectors (Pre-HVPT) mathematically coalesces into a dense, highly specific geometric boundary (Post-HVPT).6. For the pedagogical variables, we expect the $2 \times 2$ factorial analysis to demonstrate that Multi-Voice + Continuous Sentence training produces the highest Hessian precision, empirically proving that L2 learners require high-variability input to abstract phonological rules, rather than memorizing isolated acoustic templates.7.
